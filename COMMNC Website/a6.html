Nvidia is bringing its cloud gaming services to Android phones

See the photo above? That’s not Nvidia — it’s Microsoft’s xCloud, streaming Halo to an Android phone from remote internet servers powered by AMD GPUs. That’s probably why AMD rival Nvidia isn’t sitting still. The graphics giant just announced it will “soon extend” its existing GeForce Now cloud gaming service to Android phones, including flagship devices from LG and Samsung.

Unfortunately, we don’t have details like “when?” or “how much?” except that you won’t have to pay to try Nvidia’s service at first. Like the current GeForce Now for different platforms, it’ll be in beta when it starts out.

Like other mobile cloud gaming offerings, Nvidia declares you’ll need a gamepad to take full advantage of the mobile experience. They recommend gaming with a Bluetooth gamepad, such as SHIELD controller, Razer Raiju Mobile as some games will be unplayable on an Android phone without a gamepad.

It’s not far-fetched for Nvidia to bring the service to Android phones, by the way. Afterall, Nvidia’s GeForce Grid first came to the Android-based Nvidia Shield portable gaming console, and it’s been running on the Nvidia Shield TV. On Mac and PC, the invite-only beta of GeForce Now has proven to be impressive at streaming games over the internet, especially if you’ve got an excellent Wi-Fi or Ethernet connection.

However, whether it’ll work over LTE or 5G cellular connections is another question. The world is probably looking forward to when will Nvidia start making money through providing services and not just, you know, run betas forever.
